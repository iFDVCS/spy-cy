{% extends "template.html" %} {% block body %} DNA-Crypter is an application to enable anyone to encode a secret message into a DNA sequence.
An encrypted DNA sequence may be sent directly as a file, or indirectly when inserted into an organism. The only way to decrypt the message is with the unique chosen key and DNA-Crypter.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I encrypt a message?
- Encrypt. Here you insert the message, chosen key, level of encryption, and the DNA sequence to use. There is the option to upload a txt/fna file with a DNA sequence, or use the default genome provided. You will recieve a txt file with the encrypted DNA sequence.

Where can I find a DNA sequence to encode my message?
You may use any DNA sequence in a txt or fna file. The NCBI website has many genome sequences available to download. This is where the default Saccharomyces cerevisiae genome was obtained.

How do I decrypt a message?
- Decrypt. Here you upload the encrypted DNA sequence and insert the key to obtain the decrypted message.

What do the levels of encryption mean?
- This refers to the complexity of the encryption, with 1 being the lowest and 5 the highest. The more complex, the less possible it is to decrypt without the key and decryption application of DNA-Crypter.
